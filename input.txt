PYQ Trap Questions * Pralidoxime is for organophosphorus poisoning, not Datura. * Dry skin and urinary retention separate Datura from OP poisoning. * Do not give physostigmine blindly if ECG shows widened QRS or TCA co-ingestion is possible.

[09:30, 7/18/2026] Behen(SKS) 2: Management Algorithm & Treatment of Choice * First action: ABC, IV access, continuous cardiac monitoring, temperature, glucose, ECG. * Agitation/hypertension/tachycardia: benzodiazepines first line, repeated/titrated until calm and sympathetic surge improves. * Hyperthermia: rapid external cooling, sedation, IV fluids. Antipyretics are not useful for stimulant hyperthermia. * Chest pain/ischemia: aspirin if ACS suspected, benzodiazepines, nitroglycerin for vasospasm/hypertension, calcium-channel blocker if pain/hypertension persists. * Avoid pure beta-blocker as classic exam answer due unopposed alpha concern. Modern practice is nuanced, but NEET-safe response remains benzodiazepines + nitrates/CCB. * Seizures: benzodiazepines; refractory cases need ICU sedation. Treat rhabdomyolysis with fluids and monitor renal function. * QRS widening: sodium bicarbonate if sodium-channel blockade pattern. Latest Advances / Guidelines up to 2026 Recent toxicology literature is less absolute about combined alpha/beta blockers, but exam-safe guidance remains: benzodiazepines first, nitrates/CCB for cocaine chest pain, aggressive cooling for hyperthermia. PYQ Trap Questions * Bradycardia is the exception; cocaine usually causes tachycardia. * Urine screen positivity does not equal acute intoxication severity. * Do not treat stimulant hyperthermia with paracetamol as the main step. * Avoid choosing pure beta-blocker as first-line NEET answer.
